Key Market Actions
⚠
Decision required: some products do not have the minimum number of on-shelf photos for accurate training. These are competitor products, not Nestlé SKUs. Two options are available:
Nestlé Chile
1.
Pause training to capture more photos now — products in scope will be recognized, but puts the current go-live date at risk.
2.
Go live on schedule with products that have enough on-shelf photos, then run a second training round later in the project.
TELUS Recommendation · Update
Following Nestlé Chile's request to train products with fewer than the 100-photo minimum, we engaged with our vendor on technical feasibility and recommended a hybrid approach back to the market. Training at a lower count (down to approximately 80 photos) is technically possible, however accuracy cannot be guaranteed in advance, and proceeding below the threshold would require an SOW amendment excluding affected products from the 95% contractual accuracy commitment, a direction we do not recommend.

Instead, we proposed a short-term collaborative effort: Nestlé identifies the priority products for Batch 1, José Luis (with TELUS support) creates a simple field collection tool, and all 47 field users capture photos of the chocolate shelf during normal store visits. This is expected to close the photo gap within approximately one week and would extend Batch 1 go-live by the same period, in exchange for delivering at the contractually committed accuracy level and avoiding downstream recognition issues.

Awaiting Nestlé Chile's response.

Background

Nestlé has divested its Ice Cream business in Chile and Peru to Froneri International Limited. Froneri will continue operating within Nestlé's existing TELUS RE environment for approximately one year while completing their platform migration.

Nestlé remains the sole contracting and billing entity. No separate environment, contracts, or license splits are required for Froneri. This engagement is structured as Change Order #1 against the base SOW.

TELUS Deliverables
Front-end configuration of new Froneri company codes and sales organizations within the existing TELUS RE instance. No new customizations or platform modifications in scope.
Configuration testing in UAT and Production environments prior to MIT/MAT testing.
Development and execution of all MIT/MAT testing scripts. TELUS leads testing first, followed by Nestlé-identified key users.
Dedicated go-live support in coordination with the TELUS Customer Support Team.
Hypercare support post go-live for operational stability.
End-user training upon request if personnel changes result in net-new users. Additional training beyond scope requires a separate Change Order.
Project meeting participation. Formal email status updates available upon request.
Nestlé Responsibilities
Creation of the new Froneri sales organization structure and company codes within TELUS RE.
Migration of all Ice Cream master data — Territories, Customers, Products, and Pricing — to the new Froneri sales organization. Manual data entry by TELUS is explicitly out of scope.
Participate and sign off on final MIT/MAT testing.
Nestlé is responsible for overall project management activities of the implementation.
Key Constraints / Out of Scope
No new development, integrations, or customizations in scope.
Existing print templates and custom functionality used as-is.
Timeline extension into October 2026 or beyond requires a new SOW or Change Order.
